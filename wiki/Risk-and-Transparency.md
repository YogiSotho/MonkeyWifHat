# Risk and Transparency

## Why This Matters

Meme projects lose credibility fast when the joke is cleaner than the disclosures.

MonkeyWifHat can afford to be absurd in tone. It cannot afford to be vague about structure, wallets, or public claims.

## Baseline Disclosure Standard

The project should disclose:

- the intended purpose of the token
- whether there is a team allocation
- how team-controlled tokens are handled
- where official information is published
- that participation involves real risk

## Plain-Language Risk Statement

Suggested wording:

> MonkeyWifHat is a meme and community token concept intended for entertainment and participation. It does not represent equity, ownership, or a guaranteed financial return. Digital assets are volatile and high risk.

## Transparency Rules

- one set of official links
- one published source of token details
- one published allocation explanation
- no contradictory messaging across channels
- corrections posted quickly when mistakes happen

## Prohibited Messaging

Avoid statements like:

- guaranteed moon
- risk-free upside
- insider certainty
- fake partnership claims
- misleading scarcity claims

## Internal Rule

If a sentence makes the project sound more exciting by making it less accurate, the sentence does not ship.
