# Community and Content

## Principle

For a meme project, content is propulsion.

If the audience cannot remix the joke quickly, the project has no legs. MonkeyWifHat should be designed for repetition, imitation, and low-friction contribution.

## Content Pillars

- monkey-with-hat image edits
- reaction posts
- recurring catchphrases
- community hat variants
- meme contests
- creator spotlights

## Platform Use

### X

Use for:

- launch messaging
- reaction memes
- quote-post sparring
- short fragments of lore

### Telegram

Use for:

- FAQ
- moderation
- community discussion
- content submissions

### Instagram or TikTok

Use these only if the team can actually sustain short-form visual posting. Dead channels make the project look weaker, not bigger.

## Editorial Rules

- do not post false claims
- do not imply exchange listings unless confirmed
- do not manufacture endorsements
- do not invent urgency
- repeat the brand language until it becomes unmistakable

## Community Mechanics

Simple loops beat overbuilt systems.

Good examples:

- hat-of-the-week submissions
- best meme leaderboard
- profile image waves
- creator rewards tied to disclosed budgets

## Initial Asset Pack

Build these first:

- 10 launch memes
- 5 profile image variants
- 1 compact brand sheet
- 1 FAQ graphic
- 1 risk/disclaimer post
